About Me

I am an agricultural and applied economist whose research focus lies on the intersection of food economics and policy, agricultural industrial organization, and behavioral and experimental economics, with particular emphasis on agri-food safety and security. My research employs both theoretical and quantitative methods, and the main objective of my research program is to inform policymakers and food supply chain actors and provide insights to improve the agri-food system.

Currently, I work as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Agriculture at Arkansas Tech University. I received my Ph.D. in Agricultural Economics from the University of Nebraska-Lincoln, and my M.S. in Economics degree from South Dakota State University. I earned a B.S. in Economics from Khulna University of Bangladesh, where I was awarded the President’s Gold Medal for completing my undergraduate degree with the highest CGPA among all Departments in the Social Science School of Khulna University, Bangladesh.
