FOOD FRAUD

When you order seafood at a restaurant, do you get what you pay for? Scientists at Oceana, a non-profit marine conservation organization, conducted one of the largest seafood fraud investigations in 21 states of the U.S. and found that there is, on average, a 33% probability that you do not actually get what you pay for. Food Fraud can be defined as the deliberate or intentional substitution, tampering, addition, or misrepresentation of food, for economic gain. Food fraud is motivated by economic gains and is enabled by the fact that the information about the nature of the credence goods is normally asymmetric

ANTIMICROBIAL RESISTANCE & INFORMATION AVOIDANCE

In standard economics, human beings are rational agents who update their beliefs based on new available information which, in turn, can improve their knowledge and decision making. Moreover, the rational agents do not actively avoid information, except the situations in which ignorance is a strategic behavior. However, there is a growing literature in economics, psychology and neuroscience identifies countless situations in which people avoid information and prefer ignorance over knowledge.

ANTIMICROBIAL USE IN LIVESTOCK PRODUCTION

Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) is considered to be one of the most significant threats to human health worldwide. AMR is the ability of microorganisms (such as bacteria, fungi, viruses, and parasites) to resist the effects of antimicrobial drugs (such as antibiotics, antifungals, antivirals, and antimalarial) (WHO, 2014). In the United States, AMR bacteria infect around 2.8 million people each year, and cause the death of approximately 35,000; globally there are currently 700,000 deaths attributed to AMR per year, a number that is predicted to rise to 10 million by 2050 (CDC 2019). Given the current use of antibiotics in livestock production and its link to AMR, it is essential to assess the economic and social impacts of its continued use.

INFLATION TARGETING FRAMEWORK

The main feature of inflation targeting (IT) framework is the public announcement by the central bank that it will keep inflation at a numerical inflation target. A large literature addresses preconditions that a country should satisfy before adopting IT. However, there is much debate about whether satisfying these preconditions are essential. This study addresses this debate. Results of this study show that fulfillment of preconditions before IT adoption ensures effective implementation of IT in the short-run. However, countries that met all preconditions before IT adoption do not have an advantage over other IT countries that didn't meet all preconditions before IT adoption in the long-run.

GREENWASHING

Greenwashing can be defined as firm’s behavior of overestimating the true environmental quality of their product to gain market share. The main objective of this study is to determine the system-wide market and welfare effects of greenwashing under different environmental standards, such as private and public environmental standards.
